# Changelog

Important user-facing changes are documented here. This changelog is intentionally curated and high level; use the git history for commit-by-commit detail.

The format is based on [Keep a Changelog](https://keepachangelog.com/), and this project adheres to [Semantic Versioning](https://semver.org/).

## [Unreleased]
### What's New
- The audio player is now a single persistent bar that keeps playing as you browse — it no longer stops when you leave the album page. Press Play or Shuffle on an album (or play any track) and it picks up a real play queue: a new queue button opens an "Up Next" list you can see and click to jump ahead, and Shuffle now builds a fixed shuffled order you can look through rather than jumping to a random track each time. The now-playing artist is shown and links to the artist page (clicking it keeps the music going), and track tiles show their track number. Artist pages now have their own Play All and Shuffle that queue every track across all of the artist's albums. You can also minimize the player down to just the album art (with notes drifting out of it while it plays) — tap the art or the new minimize button to collapse, tap the art again to bring the full bar back — and on phones the bar now sits clear of the bottom tab bar. A close button dismisses the player entirely, clearing the queue and playback state. The minimized bubble can be grabbed and flung to the left or right edge — it lifts while held and rides its own momentum into place when you let go.
- The home dashboard now opens with your activity. A "Continue Watching" row picks up everything you're partway through (videos, shows, and books), and a "Recently Watched" row shows what you most recently finished — both ordered by when you last touched them, so the things you care about are one tap away at the top of the page.
- Books are now more than comics. EPUB and PDF books are scanned as first-class library items — their cover, title, and author are read straight from the file (PDF covers are rendered from the first page) — and open in a built-in reader. EPUBs reflow with paged or scrolling layouts and adjustable text size. PDFs get a full reader: a continuous scroll view with selectable text, zoom (fit-width, fit-one-page, +/- and pinch), an optional gapless layout, in-document search, working links, and download — plus a paged mode that turns pages with taps, swipes, and arrows. A Contents button jumps through the table of contents or PDF outline, in-book links use the brass accent, and every book remembers exactly where you left off. Comics continue to read exactly as before.
- The update checker now follows your release channel. Prismedia knows whether it is running a dev, alpha, beta, or release build and checks the container registry for newer images on that same channel, so the "update available" light only lights up for updates that actually apply to you. The sidebar shows a small channel tag (dev/alpha/beta) next to the version; release builds stay clean with no tag.
- Videos now remember where you left off. Playback position is saved as you watch, so leaving a video and coming back resumes right where you stopped, and finishing one marks it watched and counts the play. Library grids show a slim progress bar along the bottom of each cover — for videos (how much you've watched) and books (how far you've read) — and the same watched/resume state is reported to Jellyfin clients like Infuse so they stay in sync with Prismedia.
- Video, movie, and book pages share a clean progress panel (right under the description) showing your status, progress, and position (play count and resume point for video; current page for books). It has one-tap Resume and Start Over, plus a watched/read icon toggle (the glasses) that flips the watched/read flag on its own without disturbing where you left off. Start Over now reliably returns books to the first page (and clears the finished flag).
- The navigation sidebar is now yours to arrange, and your arrangement follows you everywhere. An Edit mode (the pencil at the bottom of the rail) lets you rename and reorder sections, create your own sections, drag items between them, and hide the destinations you never use — all saved to your server and shared across every device and browser instead of just the one you set it on. Hidden items reappear in Edit mode so you can switch them back on anytime, and Reset restores the original layout. The default grouping is also refreshed — media is split into Video, Images, Audio, and Books sections out of the box.
- Comics and eBooks now have their own sidebar sections, right alongside Books. The existing Books view still lists everything, while Comics narrows to your comics and manga and eBooks narrows to your books and novels — the same way Movies and Series are focused cuts of your video library.
- The mobile navigation got a glow-up: a roomier bottom bar that clears the edge of modern phones and a redesigned drawer you can flick open by swiping up from the bar (or tapping the animated chevron) and pull back down to dismiss. From the drawer you can star up to four destinations to pin them to the bottom bar — and reorder that bar with a live preview — plus hide, reorder, and regroup everything just like on desktop.
- New Auto Identify setting lets trusted plugins identify and fill new media automatically as it is scanned. Turn it on, pick which installed plugins to trust and in what order, choose which kinds of media to cover, and set a confidence threshold (90% by default) above which a match is applied without manual review. It only touches un-organized items by default and stays off until you enable it, with a note that identifying everything can make scans take longer.
- Library grids now load far faster and scroll smoothly even with hundreds of items. Covers are served as small grid-sized thumbnails and only loaded as cards come into view, so large libraries no longer stutter, lag, or pop in blank tiles while scrolling.
- Library grids can now sort and filter across the entire collection instead of just the page on screen, including a Random shuffle that reorders the whole library (with a reshuffle button) and a new "Date added (newest)" default ordering.
- Fresh instances now serve generated thumbnails as soon as the API starts, so newly scanned media no longer shows broken cover links when the cache directory did not exist yet.
- Prismedia branding now uses the cleaned primary and NSFW logos throughout the app, with a larger sidebar brand lockup and a scalable vector line mark.
- Mobile install metadata now presents Prismedia with the correct app name, theme colors, and safer icon spacing for browser and home-screen surfaces.
- Identify now leads with a collapsible "To Identify" preview (collapsed by default) on both the search and review screens, so you can confirm what you are matching: each item shows its library thumbnail — hover or drag to scrub video previews — while series and seasons show a few example episode thumbnails since they have no file of their own.
- Identify now fills a match in pieces instead of making you wait on the whole thing. Picking a result identifies the parent and opens its review right away, then a background pass walks its full tree — a series' seasons and episodes, a book's volumes and chapters, an artist's albums and tracks — and the children stream into the review as each one resolves (each as a square cover for music). Children that can't be matched show grayed out as "No match found", Accept stays disabled until the pass finishes, and progress is saved as it goes, so navigating away or refreshing the page keeps what's already resolved. Single-work items like a movie are matched on their own and no longer list their playable file as a separate child to identify.
- Images and Galleries now have a Feed view alongside Grid and List: a single, full-width column where each item shows at its real shape (tall, wide, or square) with the full title wrapped beneath it. Animated images play inline and keep playing as you scroll — the one on screen plus the next couple above and below — and a tap opens the lightbox, so the feed itself stays purely for browsing. Your filters, sort, and paging all carry over, and the choice is remembered per device.

### Added
- Multi-grid detail pages now let you collapse child sections such as Sub Galleries, Images, Seasons, Volumes, and Chapters from the section heading. Each section remembers whether you left it open or folded on that device.
- Settings now has a Transcode Cache card so prepared video never quietly fills your disk. It shows how much space the on-disk transcode/remux cache is using, lets you set a maximum size (10 GB by default; 0 for no limit), and has a one-tap "Clear cache now" button. When the cache passes the limit, the least-recently-played videos are removed automatically — always safe, since they are just re-prepared the next time you play them, and a video you are currently watching is never removed.
- The Files tab can now upload straight into a folder. The folder's "…" menu (and a watched root's) has a new "Upload files" action that opens a file picker and drops the chosen files into that folder — no need to drag and drop, which is awkward on phones.
- Sidebar sections can now be collapsed. Click a section header (Overview, Browse, Operate, or any you've made) to fold its items away, and the collapsed/expanded state is saved to your server alongside the rest of your navigation layout, so the sidebar reopens exactly how you left it on every device.
- Tags, People, and Studios now show what references them at a glance. Their library cards carry small count chips — "12" beside a video icon, "3" beside a gallery icon — for the media that credits or is tagged with them, counted live so the numbers stay accurate as your library changes.
- Tags, People, and Studios can now be filtered by whether anything references them. A new References filter in the drawer lets you show only entries that have references or only the orphaned ones nothing uses — handy for hiding empty leftovers or rounding up stale entries to trim. A new "Remove orphan tags" setting (under Library Cleanup, off by default) goes a step further and deletes tags that nothing references during each library scan, so the tag list stays tidy on its own.
- Tags, People, and Studios now sort by how much they're used by default — most-referenced first — so the things you lean on most are at the top, and the least-used drop to the bottom where they're easy to spot and trim. A new "References" sort option lets you flip the order or switch back to the usual sorts, and your choice is remembered per page.
- Tags, People, and Studios can now be created and deleted right from their library pages. An "Add" button on each page creates a new entry and drops you on its page to fill in the rest, and turning on selection lets you delete one or many at once (with a confirmation) — deleting also detaches them from any media so nothing is left pointing at a removed tag, person, or studio. A new "Orphaned" filter narrows each page to the entries nothing references yet, making empty leftovers easy to find and clean up across the whole library.
- Playing media now shows up in your device's media controls. The lock screen, notification shade, media keys, and Bluetooth controls now display the real title and artwork of what's playing — the track, artist, and album art for music, and the video (or episode) title with its thumbnail for video — and the play/pause, next/previous, and seek buttons there control Prismedia.
- The mobile navigation drawer now has the same footer as the desktop sidebar: a Changelog entry that shows the running version and channel and lights up when an update is available, plus a link to the documentation.
- Episodes opened from the Videos library now link back to the show they belong to: the breadcrumb gains the series name (Videos / Series / Episode), and the series cover appears in the episode's Details tab next to the cast so you can jump to the series in one tap.
- Each watched library now has its own Auto Identify toggle. Turn it off for a library (in Settings, or when adding one) to keep that library's media out of automatic identification even while Auto Identify is on globally — useful when you only want some libraries identified automatically. New libraries default to on.
- The Books library can now be filtered by type (Book, Comic, Manga, Novel) and by format (Comic Archive, EPUB, PDF) from the filter drawer. Picking several types or formats matches any of them, and the choice applies across the whole library, not just the page on screen.
- Music libraries now understand artists, albums, and discs. Folders are read as one of two stable layouts — `Album/Songs` or `Artist/Album/Songs` — and an **Artist** is a first-class grouping (like a gallery is for images) with its own page, metadata, and band **members** (people credited with a role such as Drummer or Vocals, modeled like a series cast). A disc subfolder inside an album (`Disc 1`, `CD2`, `Side A`, …) becomes a section of that one album, with track numbering that restarts per disc, so multi-disc and box sets stay together as a single album. The MusicBrainz plugin can now identify an artist — pulling its members, origin, formed year, and genres — in addition to albums and tracks.
- Added EPUB and PDF as single-file book formats: `.epub` and `.pdf` files scan into one book entity each (no chapter/page entities), with title/author/description read from the file (VersOne.Epub / PdfPig) and a cover taken from the EPUB's embedded image or the PDF's rendered first page (PDFtoImage). EPUB opens in a foliate-js reflowable reader (paged/scrolled, adjustable text size, comic-style tap zones, CFI resume). PDF opens in a dedicated pdf.js reader: continuous scroll with selectable text, zoom (fit-width / fit-page / buttons / pinch), gapless toggle, in-document search, internal/external links, download, an outline popup, and a paged mode (tap/swipe/arrow navigation) — resuming by page. Both stream the source file from the entities API.
- Jellyfin clients like Infuse now get a closer match to real Jellyfin: Movies appear as their own library (alongside Videos, Series, and Collections) and play with their poster and backdrop artwork; TV episodes carry their series and season artwork (logo, backdrop, thumbnail, and series poster) so "Up Next" and episode tiles show the right images, even when opened directly; a Next Up shelf surfaces shows you're partway through; and detail pages no longer error while probing for trailers, bonus features, or skip markers.
- Added first-class Movies: same-named single-file movie folders now scan as movie entities with their own Movies section, detail pages, plugin identify support, `movie.nfo` metadata, and poster/header artwork while still keeping the playable file visible in Videos.
- Added a Jellyfin-compatible API layer so clients like Infuse can discover Prismedia, sign in with configured fake users, browse video libraries, load artwork, and play through Prismedia's existing video streaming pipeline.
- Jellyfin music clients (Manet, Finamp, Symfonium, the official apps) can now stream your music: a Music library presents artists, albums, and tracks (with album/artist names, track and disc numbers, cover art, and durations), and tracks play back through Prismedia's audio pipeline — direct for common formats and transcoded on the fly otherwise. Playback position and play counts sync back the same way they do for video.
- Added a generated, human-typeable API key with Settings controls to reveal, copy, regenerate, and manage Jellyfin-compatible profile usernames with per-profile NSFW visibility.
- Added Auto Identify, an opt-in setting that runs scanned media through your chosen plugins during library scans and applies the first match that meets a configurable confidence threshold (or is an exact match) — applying the full proposal exactly like a manual identify, including child entities, relationships, and artwork — then marks the item organized. Includes an ordered, searchable plugin picker, per-kind coverage (video, galleries, images, audio, books), and an option to re-identify already-organized items.
- Added scale-appropriate library grid thumbnails: a small variant of each cover is generated at scan time and when you upload custom artwork, then served alongside the full image so the grid downloads a lightweight thumbnail for small cards and the full-resolution cover only at large card sizes. Existing libraries backfill these small variants on their next scan.
- Added library-wide grid filters that apply across the whole collection: an adaptive status filter (Read/Unread/Reading for books and comics, Watched/Unwatched/In progress for video and audio), Favorites, Organized, rating thresholds, and Unrated.
- Added Random, Date added, Rating, and Title sort options to library grids, with Random shuffling the entire library consistently across pages.
- Added Stash community scraper compatibility for identification: browse and install scrapers from the Stash Community tab on the Plugins page (Python scrapers included), then identify videos by URL or name and map scraped scenes — title, date, studio, performers, director, tags, and cover art — into Prismedia. Credited performers and the studio are proposed as reviewable cards and their posters and bios are pulled from the scraper's performer/studio lookups. Stash scrapers are always NSFW, and every entity they create or touch is marked NSFW.
- Added a typed EntityDetail action button API so route-level hero actions share consistent styling, sizing, disabled states, and mobile label behavior.
- Added Prismedia Community plugin update detection and install actions so deployed providers can move to newer compatible repository versions.
- Added global entity search results that show direct matches first, then related media for matched people, studios, and tags, with quick-open highlighted results in search and the command palette.
- Added collection creation and editing, including manual, dynamic, and hybrid modes, rule previews, visibility, and cover mode.
- Added an "Add to Collection" bulk action to library grids: turn on selection, pick one or more items, and add them to any existing collection from the selection toolbar. Collection detail pages now simply show their items, with curation handled from wherever the media lives.
- Added explicit reference-backed entity grids on people, studio, and tag detail pages so related content is listed from actual incoming relationships instead of search matches.
- Added audio playback with streaming, waveform visualization, and full transport controls.
- Added edit-mode poster and header artwork upload, drag-and-drop, and clear controls for entity detail pages.
- Added a traditional music player track list on audio library detail pages.
- Added play count tracking for audio tracks.
- Added reversible file explorer exclusions so files and folders can be skipped by library scans without deleting them.
- Added a centralized app settings registry and descriptor-driven settings UI for app-wide visibility, playback, subtitle, scan, generation, worker, and HLS defaults.
- Added a durable Identify queue so search results and metadata proposals survive navigation and backend restarts.
- Added live progress feedback while accepting Identify proposals so large cascades show the entity path currently being applied.
- Added a media wall toggle to entity grids for browsing thumbnail-only library layouts.
- Added a worker status badge to Job Control so stalled queues show when the background worker is offline.

### Fixed
- Lightbox videos now open at their intended size immediately when aspect-ratio metadata is available, and tall videos leave a small vertical inset instead of reaching the viewport edge.
- Videos in the feed and lightbox now render and play again instead of loading to a black frame, including animated gallery clips whose generated preview file is missing, while still preserving portrait and landscape aspect ratios.
- Portrait videos opened in the lightbox now keep their natural shape and use the available screen height instead of being squeezed into a wide landscape frame.
- Random sorting now reshuffles local detail-page grids such as a gallery's Sub Galleries and Images sections instead of only working on full library grids.
- Creating dynamic or hybrid collections with rules no longer trips server errors, and malformed rule payloads are now rejected as invalid collection requests.
- Comics now keep advancing reading progress across chapters, so continuing into chapter 2 or later no longer leaves Resume stuck on chapter 1.
- The empty-library dashboard action now uses Prismedia's shared primary button styling instead of the older square brass button.
- Long metadata subtitles on detail pages now wrap inside the hero area instead of running off screen on narrow layouts.
- Animated image browsing is responsive again: GIF/APNG files stay on the native image path, source-only animated clips can still play when centered in the feed, and lightbox prewarming no longer pulls full original animated sources in the background.
- The library toolbar's filter and selection rows no longer flicker in and out while you scroll. Scrolling down tucks them away once, and they stay tucked away — the bars used to spring back the instant you nudged upward, stuttering as your scroll direction wavered. The collapse toggle glows while they're hidden so it's clear how to bring them back, and the thumbnails hold their position the whole time.
- Animated image playback in feed views, image detail, and the lightbox now uses generated MP4 previews for video-like gallery images and keeps the poster visible until the first frame is ready, so large animated items no longer stall on the original source file or flash black while loading.
- The lightbox now opens video-like gallery images from the original source for full-quality playback, while feed views keep using generated previews for faster browsing.
- Comic reader page turns now decode-warm the current and nearby pages to reduce blank flashes, and the image lightbox now preloads neighboring media too.
- Animated GIFs now actually animate in the Images and Galleries Feed view. The feed was showing each one's static cover thumbnail; it now plays the original animated image inline (the one on screen plus the next couple above and below, like the inline video previews) and falls back to the still thumbnail as it scrolls out of view.
- The right-click / "…" menu in the Files tab no longer slides behind the panel next to it. Its lower entries (including Delete) could be covered by the file detail pane's search bar and view toggles; the menu now floats above everything so every action stays clickable.
- The Feed view now sticks. Switching Images or Galleries to Feed and browsing away then back used to snap the grid back to its default Grid view, because the saved view choice was being discarded as invalid on the way back in; Feed is now remembered per device alongside Grid and List.
- Re-running identify on the same item no longer duplicates its children. Identifying a container (such as a music artist) kicks off a background pass that streams in its children — albums, tracks, episodes — and going back to search and re-running (for example to switch providers) used to start a second background pass while the first was still going, so the two overwrote each other and children showed up twice. Each pass is now tied to its own run: the moment a re-search starts a new one, the old pass stops streaming, so only the current run fills in the children. Removing an item from the queue likewise stops its background pass immediately instead of letting it quietly repopulate the removed item.
- Cover art in the identify review grid no longer shows up as broken thumbnails. Provider art (such as YouTube cover art) arrives upsized to ~1000px, and loading dozens of those full-size images at once for small grid tiles made the browser drop some, leaving missing-image icons. The review now requests a right-sized preview for each tile (the full-resolution image is still what gets saved when you accept), so the grid fills in reliably.
- Re-identifying media that was already matched once now recovers instead of failing. Once a plugin saved a provider id to an item, every later identify was locked to looking that id up — so an artist's albums and tracks that were found by name on the first pass could silently come back empty on a re-run. Identify now falls back to a fresh search (the way the first pass ran) whenever the id lookup turns up nothing, so re-identifying an artist re-fills its children instead of dropping them.
- The music player's waveform no longer flickers while you scrub it with a trackpad or mouse wheel. The moving playhead and your scrubbing were fighting over the position each frame; scrubbing now holds the playhead steady and it resumes following playback once you stop.
- Music cover-art placeholders no longer spin. Tracks and albums without artwork showed a slowly rotating record icon, which made a page of them look perpetually busy; the placeholder is now still.
- Audio tracks now open correctly from the dashboard and anywhere else they're listed — their link pointed at a page that didn't exist, so clicking a recently-played or in-progress track led nowhere; it now opens the track page.
- Library grid filters now apply to your whole collection, not just the items already on screen. Filtering by NSFW / Not NSFW, Has file / No file, or Played / Unplayed is now resolved on the server across the entire library and paginated from there, so the count and pages are correct and nothing is missed when a match lives beyond the loaded page.
- Abandoned video transcodes no longer pile up and bog down the server. When you close a tab, lose connection, or leave a video paused for a while, the background ffmpeg job that was preparing it is now stopped automatically (with a hard ceiling that stops any job that has run far too long), and stopping playback now reliably halts stream-copy jobs too. Already-prepared parts stay cached, so resuming picks right back up.
- Videos play inline again in the Images and Galleries feed view. The feed used the full video player, which would silently stay on the poster whenever a clip couldn't be played directly; it now uses a lightweight muted, looping autoplay that plays reliably inline, with a slim playback progress bar along the bottom like the lightbox (a tap still opens the lightbox for full playback with sound). The feed also caps each item to the height visible below the toolbar, so a tall portrait clip stays fully on screen and narrows instead of overflowing, and Media wall mode (just the media, no captions) is now available in the feed as well as the grid.
- The Files tab no longer slows to a crawl on large libraries. Opening a folder used to scan every media file's path in the whole library to decide what to hide in SFW mode; it now looks only inside the folder you're viewing, and the file detail panel fetches just the fields it shows.
- Scanning every library at once no longer names the library it is working on in the progress text, so an NSFW library's name can't slip into the scan status while you are browsing in SFW mode. The status now reads as a count ("Scanned 2 of 5 libraries") instead.
- Dolby Vision Profile 5 videos no longer play with a purple/green color cast in the browser. Profile 5 has no standard HDR base layer, so copying it straight through to the player rendered the wrong colors; these videos are now tone-mapped to SDR like other HDR content. Dolby Vision with an HDR10- or HLG-compatible base layer (Profile 8) still streams directly for fast, light playback.
- The MusicBrainz plugin now paces its requests to stay within MusicBrainz's rate limit, so identifying an artist (which cascades through its albums and tracks) no longer overruns the provider and comes back empty. The Identify button now shows on artist and album pages, and the Identify screen labels music sections correctly ("Artists" with a mic icon, "Albums") instead of raw codes.
- Music identify now comes back with the right artwork and people: artist search results show the band photo, album covers resolve reliably (including albums whose cover only lives at the release-group level), and the artist review page now lists the band's performers with their roles — the same Credits section a series uses for its cast.
- Band members identified for an artist now actually appear on the artist page. Applying members to a music artist was silently dropped because the artist wasn't recognized as a place credits could live, so the Members section stayed empty; members are now saved on the artist and shown (re-run Identify to populate existing artists). The performers section on audio detail pages also no longer renders an empty "No credited performers yet." placeholder and now sits within the page's padding when it does show.
- Identifying an album now matches its tracks by track number instead of the on-disk filename and renames them to the canonical titles. Tracks line up with the release's track list by position — using the embedded track-number tag when present (so even alphabetically-named or messy filenames match the right track) and falling back to file order otherwise — so a file like "Artist - Album - 03 - Believer" becomes "Believer". (This updates the track's title in Prismedia; the file on disk is left untouched. Re-scan your music library to pick up track-number tags on already-scanned tracks.)
- Returning to search from an in-progress identify review now clears the children it was resolving, so re-picking a result starts cleanly instead of leaving stale, half-identified child tiles in the grid.
- Identify again resolves deep media trees end to end: a series fills in its seasons and the episodes inside them, a book fills in its volumes and the chapters inside them, and a TV season matches with the right season number again. Movies no longer show their own video file as a spurious "No match found" child. The whole tree is walked on the server and streamed into the review as it resolves, so it survives navigation and refresh and never blocks the screen while it runs.
- Identify no longer shows (or applies) a raw provider id as an item's title when a provider's detail lookup briefly fails. The MusicBrainz plugin now retries those transient hiccups so the real data loads, and as a provider-agnostic safety net any proposal whose title is just its own external id falls back to the item's existing title.
- Tapping a search or text box on iOS Safari no longer zooms the page in. Compact text fields — including the Identify search box, manual title/year inputs, and the sidebar search — kept their small text on touch devices, which triggered Safari's focus-zoom; they now hold a 16px minimum on touch while staying compact on desktop.
- EPUB and PDF book pages now show the same progress panel as comics — status, percentage, position (page for PDFs, percent for EPUBs), and Resume / Start Over with a read/unread toggle — instead of only a button that silently resumed. This makes it clear how far you've read and how to pick up or restart, especially on mobile.
- The book reader no longer flashes its toolbar open on every page turn. Turning pages in EPUBs and PDFs (by tap, swipe, arrow, or the edge arrows) leaves the toolbar alone — only a centre tap toggles it. In PDF scroll mode the toolbar no longer pops up on every scroll, and a tap now toggles it there too so it stays reachable on mobile.
- You can now seek anywhere on the timeline of a stream-copied video right from the start. These videos (where the picture is copied straight through and only the audio is converted, shown as "direct" playback) used to advertise only the part already copied as seekable, so the progress bar appeared to load far ahead of where you were watching and clicking past that point snapped back to it. Prismedia now serves the full timeline up front, so the scrubber covers the whole video immediately and a click lands where you click (seeking ahead of the copy briefly waits for it to catch up).
- The web player no longer drops a perfectly playable video into a slow transcode on the first play attempt. A momentary hiccup while a stream was still starting up (or a transient network blip) was being treated as a fatal failure and switched to a heavy re-encode — the "hit play, it fails, hit play again" behaviour. The player now retries a stalled start and only re-negotiates a transcode when the browser genuinely cannot decode the stream, so direct/stream-copied playback stays direct.
- The web player now recovers automatically when a video fails to play. If the browser turns out not to be able to decode a stream the server handed it (for example an HEVC or Dolby Vision file the browser claimed to support but can't actually render), the player now re-negotiates a compatible transcode and switches to it in place — keeping your spot — instead of stopping with a playback error. This brings problem files that "play everywhere else but not here" back to playable.
- Stream-copied (remux) videos no longer freeze or drop to a heavy transcode mid-playback. When a copy was interrupted (a brief navigation, the background reaper, a hiccup), the next request wiped the already-copied segments and started over, so the player suddenly 404'd on segments it was playing and fell back to a full re-encode. Interrupted copies now keep their segments and an actively-watched copy is never cancelled, so playback stays a smooth direct copy.
- Resuming or seeking deep into a stream-copied video now starts in a few seconds instead of stalling. The background copy is paced faster (still light — a copy is cheap), so it reaches a deep resume point quickly rather than crawling there; once a video has been fully copied, seeking anywhere is instant.
- Stream-copied (remux) videos no longer spike server CPU. The copy was written to disk as fast as the drive allowed, so a long 4K title pinned every core for the burst it took to copy the whole timeline up front. The copy is now paced — a quick initial burst for an instant buffer, then a rate cap well above playback speed — so a remuxed video plays with low, steady server load like other media servers instead of a heavy spike at the start.
- Stream-copied (remux) videos no longer intermittently fail to load with segment errors. Building the seekable timeline and starting the background copy could read playback settings at the same moment, which crashed the request and left the player unable to fetch segments; these settings are now read once up front, so remuxed playback starts reliably.
- Seeking forward and resuming partway through a transcoded movie are now fast. A forward jump (or starting from a saved resume position) used to make the server encode every second from where it had reached up to the point you landed on — so jumping ahead crawled while jumping back was instant. A jump beyond a short window now restarts the transcode right at the spot you land on, and the player's forward buffer was tuned to match so normal playback still streams smoothly.
- 4K HDR and Dolby Vision movies no longer fail to start with "a network timeout occurred while loading manifest," and stream-copied (remux) movies now start in a couple of seconds instead of stalling — which also clears up the "have to press play twice" behavior. Preparing a large movie's seekable timeline used to run a slow whole-file scan before the player received any response, which on a long 4K title took longer than the player's load timeout, so playback never began. That scan now runs in the background while playback starts immediately from a growing timeline, and the full seekable timeline swaps in moments later — so a single tap on play just works. (The player's manifest load timeout was raised to match.)
- Dolby Vision videos now stream-copy (remux) to the browser correctly instead of falling back to a heavy transcode. Profile 8 Dolby Vision files (which carry an HDR10-compatible base layer, like many 4K HDR releases) are tagged as plain HEVC, so the browser decodes the HDR10 base layer directly with near-zero server load — matching the smooth, lightweight playback other media servers give the same browser. (Tagging them as Dolby Vision instead made browsers that can't decode Dolby Vision — Chrome among them — reject the stream outright and drop to a slow CPU-bound transcode. Profile 5 Dolby Vision, which has no compatible base layer, still requires a tone-mapped transcode.)
- Stream-copied (remux) videos now keep their original surround sound. When the source audio is AAC, it is copied straight through — preserving 5.1/7.1 instead of downmixing to stereo — which also skips a needless re-encode that added CPU to every remuxed video. Other audio formats are still converted to stereo AAC for broad browser compatibility.
- Pressing play once now reliably starts the video. On some titles the first press could quietly fail (the player asked the video to start before it had loaded enough to do so) and you had to press again; the play request now holds your intent and starts the moment the video is ready, so a single press is enough.
- The video play button now shows its loading spinner while a stream is starting up, so a cold first play no longer looks like nothing happened. Previously the button flipped straight to the pause icon over a black frame while the stream prepared its first few seconds, with no spinner, so it seemed unresponsive and invited repeated play taps; it now spins until the picture is ready.
- The video progress bar no longer fills ahead of the playhead. On streams whose timeline is still being produced, the brass fill could race to the end and "complete" well before the video did, leaving the filled bar out of sync with the actual position. The played portion is now drawn from the video's real duration, so the fill, the handle, and the time readout always agree.
- Seeking in stream-copied (direct) videos is now accurate and the buffer reaches the very end. For sources with irregular keyframes, the seekable timeline handed to the player was carved into segments that didn't line up with the actual segment files, so clicking the bar jumped to the wrong spot and playback stopped short of the finish. The timeline now matches the real segments exactly, so scrubbing lands where you click and the whole video plays through.
- Seeking past the buffered part of a stream-copied (direct) video no longer snaps back into the buffer. After the transcode cache gained a size limit, a large title's seekable timeline could be trimmed away or rebuilt with a slow scan, so the player only offered the part already buffered and a click further along jumped back. Prismedia now reads the keyframe index straight from the file (near-instant, no whole-file scan) and remembers it outside the size-limited cache, so the full timeline is seekable the moment a video opens and stays seekable even after the cache is trimmed.

### Changed
- The player's Quality control is now a real manual quality picker, like other media servers. Instead of vestigial stream "levels", it offers Auto, Direct (when the original plays as-is), and a list of capped tiers — "1080p · 8 Mbps", "720p · 4 Mbps", and so on, capped to what the source actually contains. ### Removed
- Removed Stash-compatible perceptual hashing (pHash). Prismedia no longer computes video or image pHashes, the "Perceptual hash (pHash)" generation setting is gone, and fingerprint identify/contribution now relies on MD5 and oshash. The MD5 and OpenSubtitles (oshash) fingerprints are unchanged, and Stash-Box identify and fingerprint submission continue to work with them.
